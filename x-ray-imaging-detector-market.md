<p><p>X-ray imaging detectors are used in medical and industrial applications to capture images of objects or human body parts. There are two main types of detectors: direct detectors and indirect detectors. Direct detectors directly convert X-rays into electrical signals using materials like amorphous selenium, providing high image quality and efficiency. Indirect detectors use scintillator materials like cesium iodide to absorb X-rays and convert them into visible light, which is then captured by a photodiode array. Both types have their advantages and applications, and the choice depends on the specific needs of the imaging system.</p></p>
