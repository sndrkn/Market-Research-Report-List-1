<p><p>Cytotoxic drugs refer to a class of medications used to inhibit the growth of cancer cells. There are different types of cytotoxic drugs available in the market, including alkylating agents, antitumor antibiotics, antimetabolites, plant alkaloids, and others. Alkylating agents work by attaching an alkyl group to DNA, preventing cancer cells from reproducing. Antitumor antibiotics disrupt the DNA or RNA synthesis in cancer cells. Antimetabolites impede the production of necessary components for cell growth. Plant alkaloids alter the structure and function of microtubules in cancer cells. Other cytotoxic drugs utilize various mechanisms to target and destroy cancer cells.</p></p>
