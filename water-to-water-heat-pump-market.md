<p><p>A water-to-water heat pump is a type of heat pump that transfers heat energy from a water source to provide heating or cooling in a building or facility. It consists of two water loops - one for heat extraction and another for heat rejection. The heat is transferred via a refrigerant cycle, whereby the heat pump extracts heat from a water source (such as a well, lake, or river) to heat a building in winter, and then reverses the cycle to extract heat from the building and reject it into a water source for cooling in summer.</p><p>The water-to-water heat pump market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include increasing adoption of renewable energy sources, rising demand for energy-efficient heating and cooling systems, and government incentives and regulations promoting the use of heat pumps.
