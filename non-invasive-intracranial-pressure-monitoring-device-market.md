<p><p>The non-invasive intracranial pressure monitoring device market offers various types of technologies for assessing intracranial pressure without invasive procedures. Transcranial Doppler Ultrasonography utilizes sound waves to measure blood flow in the brain; Tympanic Membrane Displacement (TMD) measures eardrum movements caused by pressure changes; Optic Nerve Sheath Diameter utilizes ultrasound to measure the diameter of the optic nerve sheath as an indicator of intracranial pressure; MRI/CT scanning provides detailed images of the brain; and Fundoscopy examines the optic nerve head for signs of papilledema, indicating increased intracranial pressure.</p></p>
