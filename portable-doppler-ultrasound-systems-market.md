<p><p>Portable Doppler ultrasound systems are used for non-invasive assessment of blood flow velocity in medical settings. Directional Doppler systems detect the change in frequency of sound waves reflected by moving red blood cells to determine flow velocity and direction. They are commonly used in cardiology and vascular examinations. Non-directional Doppler systems measure the change in frequency of sound waves reflected by stationary objects to calculate flow velocity. These systems are typically used for obstetric and fetal monitoring, as well as in assessing peripheral vascular diseases.</p></p>
