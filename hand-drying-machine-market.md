<p><p>Hand drying machines are divided into two market types: hand-in and hand-under. In hand-in machines, users place their hands inside a cavity where warm air blows, drying their hands effectively. On the other hand, hand-under machines require users to place their hands under a nozzle where warm air is released, achieving the same purpose. Although both types serve the same function, the difference lies in the user experience and preference, with some individuals feeling more comfortable with one type over the other.</p></p>
