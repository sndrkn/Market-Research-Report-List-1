<p><p>Ethylene Norbornene (ENB), also known as norbornene, is an organic compound that is derived from the reaction between ethylene and cyclopentadiene. It is a colorless liquid with low toxicity and possesses excellent properties such as heat resistance, impact resistance, and flexibility. Ethylene Norbornene is primarily used as a co-monomer in the production of EPDM rubber, which is widely used in various applications such as automotive parts, electrical insulation, adhesives, and seals. EPDM rubber offers excellent resistance to heat, weather, and chemicals, making it a popular choice in the automotive industry.</p><p>The future outlook for the Ethylene Norbornene market looks promising. The increasing demand for EPDM rubber from the automotive industry, especially for the manufacturing of automotive hoses, seals, and gaskets, is expected to drive the market growth. The growing automotive industry, particularly in emerging economies, is fueling the demand for ENB.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing focus on eco-friendly products and the rising demand for EPDM rubber in roofing and construction applications are expected to boost the market growth further.
