<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The IC Packaging Substrate market consists of several types. WB CSP (Wafer-Level Chip-Scale Package) refers to packaging chips directly on a wafer, which enables high density and miniaturization. FC BGA (Flip-Chip Ball Grid Array) involves flipping the chip and connecting it to the substrate using solder balls, allowing for improved electrical performance. FC CSP (Flip-Chip Chip-Scale Package) is similar to FC BGA but with a smaller package size. PBGA (Plastic Ball Grid Array) uses plastic as the package material, making it cost-effective. SiP (System-in-Package) integrates multiple chips into a single package. BOC (Bare Chip on Board) involves directly mounting chips on a PCB without any packaging. Other markets include various packaging techniques tailored to specific requirements.</p></p>
