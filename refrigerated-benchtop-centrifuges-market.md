<p><p>Refrigerated benchtop centrifuges are widely used in laboratories for separating and analyzing substances. They come in two main types: low speed and high speed. Low speed centrifuges are designed for separating larger particles or substances, operating at a maximum speed of around 5,000-6,000 revolutions per minute (RPM). On the other hand, high speed centrifuges are built for separating smaller particles and substances, capable of reaching speeds of up to 15,000-20,000 RPM. Both types of centrifuges offer refrigeration options to maintain sample integrity, ensuring accurate results during experimentation and analysis.</p></p>
