<p><p>Nonwoven composites refer to a class of materials made by combining fibers and matrix materials without the use of weaving or knitting. They offer various advantages such as flexibility, lighter weight, and cost-effectiveness. The market for nonwoven composites can be categorized into different types based on the manufacturing process used. Meltblown composites are formed by blowing molten polymers into fine fibers, while spunbonded composites are created by extruding filaments onto a conveyor belt. Staples composites are made by assembling short fibers together, and others refer to composites produced through different methods, such as needle punching or thermal bonding.</p></p>
