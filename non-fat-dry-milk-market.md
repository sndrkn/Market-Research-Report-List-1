<p><p>Non-fat dry milk is a powdered form of milk obtained by evaporating the water content. It is widely used in various food products and beverages. The market for non-fat dry milk can be classified into three types based on the heat treatment process employed during production: low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat. Low-heat non-fat dry milk is processed at lower temperatures, resulting in a lighter flavor and better solubility. Medium-heat non-fat dry milk undergoes a slightly higher temperature treatment, providing a richer flavor and good solubility. High-heat non-fat dry milk is subjected to the highest temperature treatment, resulting in a darker color and stronger flavor, ideal for baking and cooking purposes.</p></p>
