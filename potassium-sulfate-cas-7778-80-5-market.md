<p><p>Potassium sulfate (CAS 7778-80-5) is a compound used in various applications such as fertilizers, pharmaceuticals, and industrial processes. It is produced through different market types. The Mannheim Process involves reacting potassium chloride with sulfuric acid to obtain potassium sulfate. MOP (Muriate of Potash) and Kieserite Processes also produce this compound. MOP involves extracting potassium chloride from natural deposits, while Kieserite process involves reacting magnesium sulfate with potassium chloride. Additionally, potassium sulfate can be obtained through brines processing, where it is extracted from salt lakes using evaporation techniques.</p></p>
