<p><p>The market for pellets made from recycled thermoplastics can be categorized into two types: non-biodegradable and biodegradable. Non-biodegradable thermoplastics refer to those materials that do not break down naturally over time, such as Polyethylene (PE) and Polypropylene (PP). These pellets are widely used in various industries, including packaging and automotive sectors. On the other hand, biodegradable thermoplastics, like Polylactic Acid (PLA) and Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA), are capable of decomposing naturally under specific conditions. They are increasingly being adopted in environmentally conscious applications, such as single-use plastics and compostable packaging solutions.</p></p>
