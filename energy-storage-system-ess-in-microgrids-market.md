<p><p>The energy storage system (ESS) in microgrids market encompasses two main types: grid-tied and independent. Grid-tied ESS are connected to the main power grid and provide support during peak hours, balancing energy demand and supply. They enable efficient electricity distribution and enhance the grid's reliability. Independent ESS, on the other hand, operate in isolation from the main grid, ensuring uninterrupted power supply during outages or in remote areas. These systems are self-sufficient and often rely on renewable energy sources like solar panels or wind turbines for generation.</p></p>
