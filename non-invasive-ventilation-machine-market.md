<p><p>Non-invasive ventilation machines play a crucial role in assisting patients with breathing difficulties. CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) ventilation machines provide a constant flow of air pressure to keep the airways open during sleep, primarily used for sleep apnea treatment. BiPAP (Bi-level Positive Airway Pressure) ventilation machines offer two different levels of air pressure, higher during inhalation and lower during exhalation, beneficial for patients with respiratory conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Both machines improve breathing patterns without invasive procedures, providing comfort and support for individuals with respiratory disorders.</p></p>
