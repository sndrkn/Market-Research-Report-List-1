<p><p>The amphibious vehicles market can be categorized into different types based on their propulsion systems. One common type is the screw propeller, which uses rotating blades to generate a forward thrust in water. Another type is the water jet, where a high-pressure jet of water provides propulsion. Track-based vehicles use caterpillar tracks for movement on both land and water. Lastly, there are other types that include innovative propulsion systems like air cushion and paddle wheels. These varied types cater to different needs and environments, allowing amphibious vehicles to operate effectively on both land and water.</p></p>
