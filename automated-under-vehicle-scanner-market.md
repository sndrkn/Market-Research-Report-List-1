<p><p>An Automated Under Vehicle Scanner (AUVS) is a type of security technology used to inspect the undercarriage of vehicles for hidden threats or contraband. It is typically used in high-security areas such as airports, military bases, government facilities, and border crossings. AUVS systems use a combination of high-resolution cameras, sensors, and advanced imaging software to capture detailed images of the undercarriage of vehicles as they pass over the scanner. These images are then analyzed by the software to detect any anomalies, such as foreign objects or modifications.</p><p>The market for Automated Under Vehicle Scanners is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The automated under vehicle scanner market can be classified into two types: fixed and mobile. Fixed under vehicle scanners are permanently installed in a specific location and are used to scan vehicles as they pass over the device. They are commonly installed at security checkpoints in high-security areas such as airports, government buildings, and military installations. On the other hand, mobile under vehicle scanners are portable systems that can be transported and used at various locations as needed. They are often used for temporary security measures at events, construction sites, and border crossings.</p></p>
