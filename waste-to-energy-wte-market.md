<p><p>Waste to Energy (WTE) refers to the process of converting waste materials into energy. It can be categorized into two main types: thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies involve the combustion of waste materials to produce heat, which is then converted into electricity through steam turbines. Biochemical reactions, on the other hand, use biological processes such as anaerobic digestion or fermentation to convert waste materials into biogas or biofuels. Both types of markets play a crucial role in reducing waste accumulation and providing sustainable energy sources.</p></p>
