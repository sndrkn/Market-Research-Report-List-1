<p><ul><li>Transmission Grating</li><li>Reflective Grating</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Transmission gratings are made up of thin, evenly spaced lines that allow light to pass through them. They diffract the incident light, breaking it into its spectral components. These gratings are commonly used in applications such as spectroscopy and laser beam splitting. Reflective gratings, on the other hand, reflect the incident light at different angles depending on the grating parameters. They are used for applications like laser beam shaping and wavefront analysis. Both transmission and reflective gratings find wide applications in various scientific and industrial fields.</p></p>
