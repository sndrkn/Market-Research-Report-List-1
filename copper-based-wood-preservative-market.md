<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The copper-based wood preservative market is categorized into three main types: Alkaline Copper Quaternary (ACQ), Micronized Copper Azole (MCA), and Copper Azole (CA). ACQ is a treatment process where copper compounds are mixed with an alkaline solution, providing excellent protection against decay and insects. MCA involves micronized copper particles that penetrate the wood deeply, increasing its resistance to degradation. CA is a combination of copper oxide and triazole fungicides that effectively protects wood against decay fungi and certain insects. These wood preservatives are widely used in various applications to extend the lifespan of wood products.</p></p>
