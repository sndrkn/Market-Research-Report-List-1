<p><p>Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) is a technology that tracks glucose levels in real-time throughout the day and night. It involves a small sensor that is inserted under the skin to measure glucose levels in the interstitial fluid. The data collected by the sensor is then transmitted to a receiver or a smartphone app to provide continuous readings.</p><p>The CGM market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 10.7% during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of diabetes, rising awareness of the benefits of CGM devices, and technological advancements in the field are some of the key factors driving market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the CGM market is the integration of CGM devices with insulin pumps to create a closed-loop system, also known as an artificial pancreas. This system automatically adjusts insulin delivery based on real-time glucose readings, providing better glycemic control for patients with diabetes.</p><p>Another trend is the development of non-invasive CGM technologies that eliminate the need for sensor insertion under the skin. These technologies use various techniques such as optical sensors, transdermal sensors, and radio waves to measure glucose levels without the need for blood samples.</p><p>The market analysis indicates that North America dominates the CGM market due to the high prevalence of diabetes and the presence of key market players in the region. The integration of CGM devices with insulin pumps and the development of non-invasive CGM technologies are some of the latest trends shaping the market.</p></p>

<p><p>Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) is a technology that enables individuals with diabetes to monitor their glucose levels in real-time. The market for CGM consists of three main types: transmitters, sensors, and insulin pumps. Transmitters serve as a bridge between the sensor and a display device, transmitting glucose data wirelessly. Sensors are devices that measure glucose levels in the interstitial fluid of the body. Insulin pumps, on the other hand, deliver insulin to the body based on the glucose data received from the sensors, thereby helping individuals manage their diabetes effectively.</p></p>
