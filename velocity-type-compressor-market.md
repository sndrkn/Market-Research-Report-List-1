<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Velocity type compressors are categorized into three types: axial compressors, centrifugal compressors, and Francis compressors. </p><p>Axial compressors use rotating blades to compress the gas or air in a linear motion, creating a high-velocity flow. These compressors are commonly used in aircraft engines and turbochargers.</p><p>Centrifugal compressors work by using a rotating impeller to accelerate the gas or air, converting kinetic energy into pressure. They are commonly used in industrial applications, such as oil and gas refining.</p><p>Francis compressors combine features of both axial and centrifugal compressors. They have curved blades that allow for a combination of high pressure and high flow capacity. They are typically used in hydroelectric power plants.</p></p>
