<p><p>The hemodialysis vascular graft market encompasses various types of graft materials, including polyester, polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), polyurethane, and biological materials. Polyester grafts are made of a synthetic fabric that provides durability and resistance to infection. PTFE grafts are constructed from a fluoropolymer material known for its high strength and low friction properties. Polyurethane grafts offer flexibility and biocompatibility. Biological materials, such as human or animal tissue, can also be used as grafts. Each type of graft material has its own advantages and suitability for different patient needs in hemodialysis procedures.</p></p>
