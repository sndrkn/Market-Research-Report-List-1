<p><p>Brain tumor diagnosis and treatments involve using various techniques to identify and treat abnormal growths in the brain. The diagnosis process often starts with neurological examinations, including physical and mental assessments. Imaging tests such as MRI, CT scans, and PET scans are then conducted to obtain detailed images of the brain. Biopsy, a surgical procedure, is performed to collect tissue samples for laboratory testing and to determine the tumor's characteristics.</p><p>There are several treatment options for brain tumors, including surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted drug therapy, and immunotherapy. The choice of treatment depends on factors such as the type, size, and location of the tumor, as well as the patient's age and overall health. Surgery is typically the primary treatment, aiming to remove as much of the tumor as possible. Radiation therapy uses high-energy beams to kill cancer cells, while chemotherapy and targeted drug therapy use drugs to destroy tumor cells. Immunotherapy helps stimulate the body's immune system to fight against cancer cells.</p><p>The brain tumor diagnosis and treatments market is experiencing significant growth due to various factors.
