<p><p>The medical lithotripsy device market is divided into two types: intracorporeal lithotripsy and extracorporeal lithotripsy. Intracorporeal lithotripsy involves the use of devices that are inserted within the body to break down kidney stones or gallstones. This type of lithotripsy is often performed through endoscopic procedures. On the other hand, extracorporeal lithotripsy entails the use of external shock waves to fragment kidney or urinary stones from outside the body. This type of lithotripsy is a non-invasive procedure commonly used for kidney stone treatment.</p></p>
