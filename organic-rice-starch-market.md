<p><p>The organic rice starch market is classified into three types: food grade, pharmaceutical grade, and cosmetic grade. Food grade refers to the use of rice starch as an ingredient in food products, such as sauces, soups, and baked goods, to provide thickening, stabilizing, or texturizing properties. Pharmaceutical grade involves the utilization of rice starch in the pharmaceutical industry for various purposes, including as a binder, filler, or disintegrant in tablets and capsules. Cosmetic grade denotes the application of rice starch in cosmetic products, such as lotions, creams, and powders, to absorb excess oil, enhance texture, or provide a matte finish.</p></p>
