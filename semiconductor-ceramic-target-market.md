<p><p>Semiconductor ceramic targets are a critical component in the production of electronic devices. Planar targets are flat plates made of semiconductor ceramics, used primarily in the sputtering process to deposit thin films on substrates. They are cost-effective and suitable for large scale production. On the other hand, rotary targets are cylindrical in shape and offer higher material utilization efficiency. These targets are commonly used in smaller-scale production or research settings, where material waste reduction is crucial. Both types cater to different production needs in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
