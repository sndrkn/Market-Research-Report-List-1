<p><p>Impact Modified Styrene Methyl Methacrylate (I-SMMA) is widely used in various molding processes. Calendering molding involves shaping the material between two rotating rollers, creating thin sheets used in applications like graphic films or laminates. Extrusion molding, using a heated barrel and screw, forces the material through a die to create profiles, pipes, or films. Blow molding employs air to expand the material into hollow shapes, commonly used in packaging bottles or containers. Injection molding injects molten I-SMMA into a mold cavity, producing complex shapes with high efficiency, commonly used in automotive or consumer goods industries.</p></p>
